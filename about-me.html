About Me

I am a first-year (very soon to be second-year) undergraduate student of Computing at the University of Gloucestershire. My biggest interest within the realm of computing is full-stack web development although I am more partial to the backend side of things. I'm currently learning and experimenting with Laravel, which I'm finding to be very comfy to use and develop with, and a nice breath of fresh air to the usual full-JavaScript stack that so many evangelise these days.

Outside of the subject my biggest interests lie in Bushcraft, reading and the guitar - they go together a lot more than you would expect! In my view, the natural world is sustaining far too much damage, so much so that even in my rural village area I've taken to taking a bin-bag out with me because the obscene amount of litter I walk past on the way to the local woods is downright infuriating. Although my preferences for privacy point me towards search engines such as DuckDuckGo or, even better, a random Searx instance, I highly recommend and will unashamedly evangelise for the usage of Ecosia. They are not quite as private as I would like (although far, far better than the likes of Google), but they are an extremely ethical company that is genuinely making a difference, with around 98 million trees planted at the time of writing this. If there is anything you take away from this mess of a website, I hope it is a spark of interest in using Ecosia.
